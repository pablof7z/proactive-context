# Episode: Positioning thesis: from memory/verbatim to judgment-compounding

## Prior State

The project was externally positioned as 'Live vector index + RAG over your local markdown files' (stale v0.1 framing). Internally the wiki-model and capture→inject loop were the real architecture, but no clear thesis articulated what made the project special vs. the commodity 'memory' category.

## Trigger

User rejected 'memory' as a crowded/weak anchor (line 266), then rejected the verbatim-citation differentiator as too narrow (line 755). Research agents confirmed feature-level distinctiveness is low (2/10 red team) and every individual capability exists elsewhere. User pushed for the Vervaeke-adjacent spirit: human touchpoints carry irreplaceable perspective.

## Decision

The positioning thesis shifts from a feature-level differentiator ('it shows its work' / verbatim-citation) to a meaning-level inversion: 'your judgment is the scarce, appreciating asset; model output is the disposable input.' Verbatim-citation is demoted from headline to proof-of-sincerity — the mechanism that proves the system means it, not the reason to care. Local-first and wiki-of-guides become downstream consequences of the stance, not standalone selling points.

## Consequences

- The README must lead with 'judgment compounding' as the job-to-be-done, not verbatim-citation as the mechanism
- All tweets/launch copy should derive from the inversion (output is cheap; your taste is the asset), not from 'it quotes verbatim'
- The 'regenerable spec that can't fabricate a requirement' idea in citation-anchored-capture.md is the roadmap wedge that can sharpen the thesis further once built, but cannot be claimed as present-tense
- The research finding that feature-level distinctiveness is ~2/10 is now accepted project knowledge — any future positioning must clear this bar

## Open Tail

- User has not yet confirmed the 'judgment is the asset' reframe — the final probe tweets (A/B/C) are pending feedback
- If the inversion thesis is confirmed, the README lead, tagline, and all launch copy need rewriting around it
